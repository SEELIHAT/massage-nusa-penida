Kelingking Beach Beach, also known as Kelingking Beach, is a stunning hidden beach located on the island of Nusa Penida in Indonesia. It is famous for its iconic rock formation that resembles the shape of a T-Rex dinosaur, which has made it a popular spot for visitors seeking Instagram-worthy photos. The beach is located on the southwestern coast of Nusa Penida and is accessible via a steep and challenging hike down the cliffside.

The hike to Kelingking Beach is not for the faint of heart, as it involves navigating steep and rocky terrain. However, the effort is well worth it, as the beach offers breathtaking views of the turquoise waters below and the surrounding cliffs. The beach itself is relatively small and is made up of golden sand, making it a picturesque spot for sunbathing and swimming.

While visitingKelingking Beach, it's important to take caution and watch your step, as the cliffs can be dangerous. It's also advisable to bring plenty of water and wear sturdy footwear for the hike down and back up. Despite the challenges, Kelingking Beach is a must-visit destination for those exploring Nusa Penida, offering a truly unforgettable experience amidst stunning natural beauty.
