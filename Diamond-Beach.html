Location:
Diamond Beach is situated on the southwestern coast of Nusa Penida, an island known for its stunning landscapes, crystal-clear waters, and unique rock formations.
Scenery and Environment:
Located on the island’s eastern coast, it is famous for its dramatic cliffs, crystal-clear water, and distinctive limestone rock formations that rise from the ocean like diamonds, which inspired the beach's name. Until a few years ago, Diamond Beach was difficult to access. Today, visitors can reach the shore by descending a staircase carved directly into the cliff. The walk down offers breathtaking views of the coastline and is an attraction in itself. The beach is known for its soft white sand and vibrant turquoise water. During calm conditions, it is a beautiful place to relax, take photographs, and enjoy the tropical scenery. However, the waves and currents can be strong, so visitors should be cautious when entering the ocean. One of the most remarkable features of Diamond Beach is the contrast between the towering cliffs, lush green vegetation, and the bright blue sea. In the morning, sunlight illuminates the beach and creates stunning colors that attract photographers from around the world.

Accessibility:
Access to Diamond Beach can be challenging due to its remote location and the rugged terrain of Nusa Penida. Visitors may need to hike or take a scooter ride on narrow paths to reach the beach. It's recommended to hire a local guide or join a tour to navigate the area safely.
Amenities:
Diamond Beach is relatively undeveloped compared to other popular beaches, so amenities such as restaurants, shops, and restrooms may be limited. Visitors should bring their own supplies, including water, snacks, and sun protection.
Best Time to Visit:
The viewpoint overlooks the rugged coastline of Nusa Penida, offering panoramic views of dramatic cliffs, rolling waves, and the endless blue waters of the Indian Ocean.
